Are you struggling to get the most out of your agile team? Do you have a brand new crew who are just getting up to speed? Or do you simply want to learn more about BDD?

After training hundreds of people at the BBC in Behaviour Driven Development principles, and seeing huge success, for the first time Matt Wynne (author of The Cucumber Book) and Chris Parsons (well regarded speaker and trainer) are opening up their Ruby and Cucumber training courses to the wider public.

Come and equip your team to really succeed with BDD, without just paying lip-service to the ideas. We'll take developers and product owners through several days of intense hands-on workshops, and teach them to avoid the misunderstanding and gotchas teams so often fall into.

Course schedule

 Day 0: Just enough Ruby

A ruby course specifically designed for programmers from other languages, to teach you the basics. If you've not done much Ruby before, then this day is for you. we'll get you up to speed fast on the basics that you'll need to know to comfortably use Cucumber effectively.

Topics covered:

  • Everything is an object
  • Data types and structures
  • Flow, blocks and closurs
  • Baking your own: classes and modules

 Day 1: BDD

Matt's blurb

 Day 2: BDD with Cucumber

By the end of the day, you'll know what Cucumber is (and isn't), how to set it up and how it's best used. We'll take you through the different levels of behaviour in any application and how to properly capture your team's conversation in your features.

Topics covered:

  • The lie of the land: the simplest possible cucumber project
  • Adding features: the basic BDD cycle
  • Writing features right: layers of abstraction
  • Refactoring existing features: tools and tips

About Chris and Matt

Matt Wynne is one of the world's leading BDD practitioners. A programmer, coach, trainer and popular international speaker, he's the author of The Cucumber Book and a member of the Cucumber core team.

Chris Parsons is an itinerant agilist, programmer, writer and trainer. He consults in technical architecture and agile management, and trains and speaks at conferences in the USA and Europe on Ruby, Cucumber, being agile, code quality and programmer education. To keep his hand in, he also works on high profile web applications such as this one.
