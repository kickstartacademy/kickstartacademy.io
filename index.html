Imagine a software team where everyone understood exactly what they need to do.

Behaviour-Driven Development (BDD) is revolutionising software development by turning traditional processes on their head. Instead of waiting until the code has been written to start testing it, we bring testers into the heart of the process, collaborating with product owners and developers to create specifications that make sense to everyone on the team. Developers then automate these specifications using Cucumber, ensuring the behaviour is automatically checked in every release.

BDD deals with the trickiest part of working in a team: communication between people. Cucumber captures communicated requirements in a form that ensures your application stays working over time.

Learn BDD and Cucumber in a hands-on practical workshop from two of the world’s leading practitioners.

About Chris and Matt

Matt Wynne is one of the world's leading BDD practitioners. A programmer, coach, trainer and popular international speaker, he's the author of The Cucumber Book and was invited to join the Cucumber core team in 2009.

Matt has worked with large organisations like the BBC. He also wrote and runs Relishapp.com and is the official host for RSpec’s documentation. He conceived, organised and ran Bootstrapd, a non-profit conference for bootstrapped startups.

Matt has spoken at Agile 2009, XP day London, Agile Cambridge, Scottish Ruby Conference, Gotham Ruby Conference, QCon NYC, Software Practice Advancement, Software Craftsmanship UK, and Turku Agile day.

Chris Parsons is an itinerant agilist, programmer, writer and trainer. He consults in technical architecture and agile management and was the owner of the highly respected Ruby shop, Eden Development, which led the way in applying the BDD process to client projects. More recently, Chris designed the hosting infrastructure and was one of the leading developers on e-petitions, a high profile web application that was written using BDD techniques and had 17 million visits in its first year. His clients have included large organisations such as the BBC and GOV.UK, the new UK government portal launched in October 2012, as well as a number of startups.

Chris trains and speaks internationally on Ruby, Cucumber, being agile, code quality and programmer education at conferences including Rubyconf, the Scottish Ruby Conference, and Software Craftsmanship North America and Software Craftsmanship UK.
